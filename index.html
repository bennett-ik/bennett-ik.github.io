Introduction

About Me

I am currently pursuing an integrated M.S./Ph.D. program in the Department of Energy and Chemical Engineering at UNIST (Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology), within the JW Energy Lab. I received my B.S. in Chemistry from Kwangwoon University.

My research interests lie at the intersection of electrocatalysis and renewable energy, with a focus on bias-free green hydrogen production via biomass valorization, solar-driven water splitting (PEC and PV-EC systems), single-atom and perovskite electrocatalyst synthesis, and MEA-based electrochemical systems.

Work

Research

01
Electrochemical Furfural Oxidation
Investigation of electrochemical oxidation pathways of furfural to value-added chemicals such as furoic acid. Focus on developing efficient electrocatalysts under alkaline conditions and elucidating reaction mechanisms at the electrode–electrolyte interface.
02
Catalyst Synthesis — Single-Atom & Perovskite
Design and synthesis of advanced heterogeneous catalysts including single-atom catalysts (SACs) and perovskite-structured oxides. Exploration of structure–activity relationships to optimize active site density, selectivity, and stability for electrochemical applications.
03
Perovskite Solar Cell-Based PEC & PVEC
Integration of perovskite solar cells with photoelectrochemical (PEC) and photovoltaic-electrochemical (PVEC) systems for solar-driven water splitting and chemical synthesis. Targeting high solar-to-chemical conversion efficiency through device architecture optimization.
